📜 Welcome to the Emporium

Welcome, adventurer! Before you browse our wares, take a moment to familiarise yourself with how trade works in the realm. Gold doesn't grow on trees — unless you're a Druid, in which case, please put it back.

Purchasing items is simple: find what you need, note the cost, and hand over your coin to the Dungeon Master at the start of your session or at an appropriate in-game moment (entering a town, visiting a market, etc.). Your DM will deduct the amount from your character's gold total.

All prices are listed in gold pieces (gp), silver pieces (sp), or copper pieces (cp). Use the exchange rate table below to convert between them.

🪙 Currency & Exchange Rates

Three coins are in common use across the realm. The gold piece is the standard of wealth — with one gold piece a character can buy a bedroll, 50 feet of rope, or feed a goat for a month. The silver piece is the coin of commoners, while the copper piece buys only the smallest luxuries.

⚖️ Simplified Weight System

Most small items have no weight rating — a candle, a coin, a piece of chalk won't slow you down. Heavier items are assigned a Weight Rating based on their bulk:

Carry capacity: You can carry items up to a total Weight Rating of 4 + your Strength modifier. For example, a character with a +2 Strength modifier can carry items totalling Weight Rating 6.
Common sense rule: Items without a weight rating still take up physical space. If your character is carrying an absurd quantity of any item, the Dungeon Master may apply real-world logic and assign appropriate consequences. Don't stuff 500 candles in your pocket.

🛡️ Armour
↗ More info on armour (Roll20 Compendium)

Armour protects you from harm by raising your Armour Class (AC). Heavy armour may impose disadvantage on Stealth checks and requires minimum Strength scores to wear without penalty. You must be proficient in armour to wear it without disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks involving Strength or Dexterity.
